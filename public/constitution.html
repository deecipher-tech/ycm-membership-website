Preamble

We, the youth of Nigeria, recognizing the need for positive change, civic engagement, and national development, do hereby establish this Constitution for the Youth Concessive Movement (YCM). United in our commitment to empower Nigerian youth, foster leadership, and promote community development, we pledge to uphold the principles of integrity, inclusivity, and progress as outlined in this governing document.

Declaration of Purpose

This Constitution establishes the framework for governance, membership, and operations of the Youth Concessive Movement, guiding our collective efforts toward youth empowerment and national development.

I

Article I: Name and Headquarters

Section 1: Name

The organization shall be known as the "Youth Concessive Movement," hereinafter referred to as "YCM" or "the Movement."

Section 2: Headquarters

123 Youth Avenue, Central District, Abuja, Nigeria

Section 3: Regional Offices

YCM may establish regional offices in each of the 36 states of Nigeria and the Federal Capital Territory as needed for effective operations. Each regional office shall operate under the directives of the National Executive Council.

II

Article II: Vision, Mission, and Objectives

Vision

A Nigeria where every young person has the opportunity, skills, and platform to contribute meaningfully to society and drive positive change in their communities.

Mission

To empower Nigerian youth through comprehensive civic education, practical leadership development, and meaningful community engagement.

Motto

"Empowered Youth, Progressive Nation"

Core Objectives

Promote civic education and political awareness
Develop leadership skills and capacity
Foster unity and national integration
Provide platforms for youth participation
Facilitate skills acquisition and entrepreneurship
Engage in community development projects
Advocate for youth-friendly policies
Promote digital literacy and innovation
III

Article III: Membership

Section 1: Eligibility

  • Nigerian citizens and residents aged 16-35 years
  • Individuals who subscribe to YCM's vision and objectives
  • Willing to abide by this Constitution and Code of Conduct
  • Persons of good character and reputation

Section 2: Membership Categories

Regular Members

Youth aged 16-35 who meet eligibility requirements

Associate Members

Individuals above 35 who support YCM objectives

Honorary Members

Distinguished persons conferred by the National Executive Council

Section 3: Rights and Privileges

Right to vote in elections and referendums
Right to hold elective positions (subject to eligibility)
Access to YCM resources, training, and opportunities

Section 4: Duties and Responsibilities

Uphold and promote YCM's vision and objectives
Participate actively in YCM activities and programs
Pay prescribed dues and levies (if any)
IV

Article IV: Organizational Structure

Section 1: National Level Structure

National Convention

Supreme governing body

National Executive Council

Day-to-day administration

Board of Trustees

Custodians of YCM legacy

Standing Committees

Specialized functional committees

National Secretariat

Administrative headquarters

Section 2: State Level

State Congress

Highest state decision-making body

State Executive Committee

State-level administration

State Secretariat

State administrative office

Section 3: Local Government Level

LGA Chapter

Local government area chapter

LGA Coordinator

Local government coordinator

Ward Representatives

Representatives at ward level

Structural Hierarchy

All lower-level structures operate under the guidance and supervision of higher-level structures, ensuring unity of purpose and coordinated action across all levels of the organization.

V

Article V: National Executive Council

Section 1: Composition

Position Responsibilities Term
National Chairman
 Chief executive officer and spokesperson 2 years
National Secretary
 Administration and records management 2 years
National Treasurer
 Financial management and accountability 2 years
Publicity Secretary
 Media relations and communications 2 years
Ex-Officio Members (3)
 Advisory and special assignments 2 years

Section 2: Term of Office

Members of the National Executive Council shall hold office for a term of two (2) years and may be re-elected for one additional consecutive term.

Section 3: Powers and Functions

  • Implement decisions of the National Convention
  • Manage day-to-day affairs of YCM
  • Prepare and present annual reports and budgets
