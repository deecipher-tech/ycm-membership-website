Constitution

The governing document of Youth Concessive Movement

Adopted: January 1, 2025

Preamble

We, the youth of Nigeria, recognizing the need for positive change, civic engagement, and national development, do hereby establish this Constitution for the Youth Concessive Movement (YCM). United in our commitment to empower Nigerian youth, foster leadership, and promote community development, we pledge to uphold the principles of integrity, inclusivity, and progress as outlined in this governing document.

Article I: Name and Headquarters

Section 1: Name

The organization shall be known as the "Youth Concessive Movement," hereinafter referred to as "YCM" or "the Movement."

Section 2: Headquarters

The national headquarters of YCM shall be located at:

123 Youth Avenue, Central District, Abuja, Nigeria

Section 3: Regional Offices

YCM may establish regional offices in each of the 36 states of Nigeria and the Federal Capital Territory as needed for effective operations.

Article II: Vision, Mission, and Objectives

Section 1: Vision

A Nigeria where every young person has the opportunity, skills, and platform to contribute meaningfully to society and drive positive change in their communities.

Section 2: Mission

To empower Nigerian youth through comprehensive civic education, practical leadership development, and meaningful community engagement, creating a generation of proactive citizens committed to sustainable national development.

Section 3: Core Objectives

  • To promote civic education and political awareness among Nigerian youth
  • To develop leadership skills and capacity in young Nigerians
  • To foster unity and national integration among youth from diverse backgrounds
  • To provide platforms for youth participation in governance and decision-making
  • To facilitate skills acquisition and entrepreneurship development
  • To engage in community development projects and social initiatives
  • To advocate for youth-friendly policies and legislation
  • To promote digital literacy and technological innovation

Article III: Membership

Section 1: Eligibility

Membership in YCM shall be open to:

  • Nigerian citizens and residents aged 16-35 years
  • Individuals who subscribe to the vision and objectives of YCM
  • Those willing to abide by this Constitution and YCM Code of Conduct
  • Persons of good character and reputation

Section 2: Membership Categories

  • Regular Members: Youth aged 16-35 who meet eligibility requirements
  • Associate Members: Individuals above 35 who support YCM objectives
  • Honorary Members: Distinguished persons conferred by the National Executive Council
  • Corporate Members: Organizations that support YCM's vision

Section 3: Rights and Privileges

  • Right to participate in YCM activities and programs
  • Right to vote in elections and referendums
  • Right to hold elective positions (subject to eligibility)
  • Access to YCM resources, training, and opportunities
  • Receive YCM publications and communications

Section 4: Duties and Responsibilities

  • Uphold and promote YCM's vision and objectives
  • Abide by this Constitution and YCM Code of Conduct
  • Participate actively in YCM activities and programs
  • Pay prescribed dues and levies (if any)
  • Maintain good conduct and represent YCM positively

Article IV: Organizational Structure

Section 1: National Level

The national structure shall consist of:

  • National Convention (Supreme Governing Body)
  • National Executive Council (NEC)
  • Board of Trustees
  • Standing Committees
  • National Secretariat

Section 2: State Level

Each state shall have:

  • State Congress
  • State Executive Committee
  • State Coordinators
  • State Secretariat

Section 3: Local Government Level

Each Local Government Area shall have:

  • LGA Chapter
  • LGA Coordinator
  • Ward Representatives

Article V: National Executive Council

Section 1: Composition

The National Executive Council shall consist of:

  • National Chairman
  • Vice Chairman
  • National Secretary
  • Assistant Secretary
  • National Treasurer
  • Financial Secretary
  • Publicity Secretary
  • Legal Adviser
  • Welfare Officer
  • Three (3) Ex-Officio Members

Section 2: Term of Office

Members of the National Executive Council shall hold office for a term of two (2) years and may be re-elected for one additional term.

Section 3: Powers and Functions

  • Implement decisions of the National Convention
  • Manage day-to-day affairs of YCM
  • Prepare and present annual reports and budgets
  • Appoint standing committee members
  • Establish policies and guidelines for YCM operations

Article VI: Elections and Appointments

Section 1: Electoral Commission

An Independent Electoral Commission shall be constituted to conduct all YCM elections. The Commission shall consist of five (5) members appointed by the Board of Trustees.

Section 2: Eligibility for Election

To be eligible for elective office, a member must have been in good standing for at least one (1) year and must meet specific requirements for each position as outlined in the Electoral Guidelines.

Section 3: Voting Procedures

All elections shall be conducted through secret ballot. Electronic voting may be adopted where feasible and secure. Simple majority shall determine the winner in all elections.

Article VII: Financial Provisions

Section 1: Sources of Funds

  • Membership dues and levies (if applicable)
  • Donations and grants from individuals and organizations
  • Fundraising activities and events
  • Government and international development grants
  • Income from YCM projects and enterprises

Section 2: Financial Management

  • All funds shall be deposited in YCM bank accounts
  • Proper accounting records shall be maintained
  • Annual audits shall be conducted by independent auditors
  • Financial reports shall be presented to the National Convention

Section 3: Expenditure Authorization

All expenditures shall be authorized in accordance with approved budgets. The National Treasurer and National Chairman shall jointly authorize expenditures above specified limits as determined by the National Executive Council.

Article VIII: Meetings

Section 1: National Convention

The National Convention shall hold once every two (2) years. Special conventions may be convened by the National Executive Council or upon written request of two-thirds of state chapters.

Section 2: National Executive Council Meetings

The National Executive Council shall meet quarterly. Emergency meetings may be convened by the National Chairman or upon request of one-third of NEC members.

Section 3: Quorum

  • National Convention: One-third of registered delegates
  • National Executive Council: Two-thirds of members
  • State Congress: One-third of registered members

Article IX: Amendments

Section 1: Amendment Procedure

This Constitution may be amended by a two-thirds majority vote of delegates at the National Convention. Proposed amendments must be submitted in writing to the National Secretary at least sixty (60) days before the Convention.

Section 2: Review Commission

A Constitution Review Commission may be established every five (5) years to review this Constitution and recommend necessary amendments to ensure its relevance and effectiveness.

Article X: Dissolution

Section 1: Procedure for Dissolution

YCM may be dissolved by a resolution supported by three-quarters of registered members voting in a national referendum. Such dissolution shall be conducted under the supervision of the Board of Trustees.

Section 2: Distribution of Assets

Upon dissolution, all assets remaining after settlement of liabilities shall be transferred to another non-profit organization with similar objectives, as determined by the Board of Trustees.
